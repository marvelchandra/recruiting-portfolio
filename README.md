## Sourcing Depth: Where I Find Talent Others Don't

Most recruiters start and end with LinkedIn. I use it too — but it's rarely where the best passive candidates for deep tech roles are found.

### AI/ML & Research Roles

| Channel | What I look for |
|---|---|
| **ArXiv** | Author pages for papers in target research areas (transformers, RL, vision, etc.) |
| **Papers With Code** | Contributor lists on benchmark-leading implementations |
| **GitHub** | Commit histories on major OSS ML frameworks (PyTorch, JAX, vLLM, etc.) |
| **Google Scholar** | Citation networks — who's citing the papers that matter to your team |
| **Kaggle** | Top-ranked competitors in relevant competition tracks |
| **University AI labs** | PhD/PostDoc pipelines at Stanford, Berkeley, MIT, CMU, Caltech, Cornell |
| **Conference proceedings** | NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR author lists |

### Semiconductor & Hardware

| Channel | What I look for |
|---|---|
| **IEEE Xplore** | Author publication history in target domains (PCIe, SerDes, ASIC) |
| **HotChips / ISSCC** | Presenter and panelist lists |
| **LinkedIn (filtered)** | Past company alumni from Broadcom, Marvell, Intel, AMD, Qualcomm, Nvidia |
| **GitHub** | Open-source HDL contributors (SystemVerilog, VHDL, Chisel) |
