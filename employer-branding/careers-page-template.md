# Careers Page Template — Building a Page That Sells Before You Source

**Maintained by:** Chandra Buduri · [linkedin.com/in/chandrabuduri](https://linkedin.com/in/chandrabuduri)

> A careers page is the first destination for any candidate who does their homework before responding to your outreach. It's also where inbound candidates land. Most startup careers pages are placeholder pages that do neither. This template gives you a production-ready structure you can fill in and launch in a day.

---

## Page Structure

Copy this structure into your website CMS, Notion, or build it as a standalone page.
Recommended tools: Webflow, Notion (simple), Ashby/Greenhouse careers page (integrated with ATS).

---

### Section 1: Hero

**Goal:** Capture attention and communicate what the company does in under 10 seconds.

```
[Company Logo]

[One-line mission statement]
Example: "We build the infrastructure that powers the next generation of protein research."

[One-line stage/traction signal]
Example: "Series A · Backed by [Notable VC] · Used by [N] of the top [Y] research labs"

[CTA Button: "See Open Roles" → scrolls to roles section]
```

**What NOT to do:**
- "Join our team!" (says nothing)
- "We're hiring!" (every company is)
- Generic hero image of people in an open office
- Video that auto-plays

### Section 2: The Problem We're Solving

**Goal:** Give candidates the context to decide if this mission resonates.

```
## Why [Company] Exists

[Paragraph 1: The problem]
What's broken in the world that you're fixing? Be specific.
Who experiences this problem? What does it cost them?

[Paragraph 2: Your approach]
Why is your approach different from what's been tried before?
What's the insight or technical bet at the core of your company?

[Paragraph 3: The moment]
Why is now the right time? What's changed that makes this possible?
What happens if nobody solves this?
```

**Example (filled in):**
```
## Why [Company] Exists

Drug discovery takes 12–15 years and costs over $2B per approved therapy —
and 90% of candidates still fail in clinical trials. The bottleneck isn't money or effort.
It's that we don't understand how proteins fold and interact well enough to design
molecules that work before we make them.

We're building a foundation model for molecular biology — trained on every publicly
available protein structure and interaction dataset, fine-tuned on proprietary wet lab data
from our research partners. The goal is to compress the early-stage discovery cycle
from years to weeks.

AlphaFold proved that deep learning can crack protein structure. What it didn't solve
is the broader problem of molecular interaction prediction at scale. That's the problem
we're working on — and the compute and data environment to do it are finally here.
```

### Section 3: How We Work

**Goal:** Give candidates a real sense of what it's like to work here — including what's hard.

```
## How We Work

[3–5 specific statements about your working culture.
Be honest. Include at least one thing that's challenging.]

---

**We move fast and change direction when the data says to.**
We shipped [N] major product changes last quarter. Some of them were mistakes.
We'd rather learn fast than plan slowly.

**You'll have real ownership.**
There are no PMs telling engineers what to build. If you're hired to own something,
you own it — including the decisions about how to build it.

**We're small and intend to stay lean.**
We're [N] people. We don't plan to be 200. Every hire raises the bar or it doesn't happen.

**The work is genuinely hard.**
We don't have all the answers. Some of the problems we're working on don't have
known solutions. If that's frustrating to you, this probably isn't the right fit.
If it's energizing, you'll love it here.

**We communicate in writing.**
Async-first. Major decisions are documented. You should be comfortable writing
clearly and reading carefully.
```

### Section 4: The Team

**Goal:** Make the company feel real and human. Candidates want to know who they'd be working with.

```
## The Team

[Photo grid — real photos, not headshots from 2015]

[Name] — [Title]
[1–2 sentences: what they did before, what they own now]
Example: "Previously led the ML infrastructure team at Waymo.
At [Company], owns everything from model training to serving."

[Name] — [Title]
[1–2 sentences]

[Name] — [Title]
[1–2 sentences]

[Show 5–8 people — not the whole company, not just the founders]
[Include a mix of levels and functions if possible]
```

**What NOT to do:**
- Only show the founders (makes the company look like a 2-person operation)
- Use professional headshots that look like LinkedIn profile photos from 2018
- Include bios that are just LinkedIn summaries copy-pasted

### Section 5: Benefits & Compensation

**Goal:** Be transparent. Candidates who can't see the comp range self-select out unnecessarily — or waste everyone's time by applying and then declining.

```
## What We Offer

**Compensation**
We pay competitively for our stage.
Base salary ranges are included in every job description.
Equity: [X]% of the company is allocated to the option pool.
We explain how equity works in every recruiter conversation.

**Health & Wellness**
- Medical, dental, and vision — [X]% employer-covered
- [Any additional wellness benefits]

**Remote & Flexibility**
- [Remote / Hybrid (N days/week in [city]) / Onsite]
- [Home office stipend: $X one-time]
- [Internet stipend: $X/month]

**Time Off**
- [Unlimited PTO / N weeks PTO] — [note if there's a minimum encouraged]
- [Paid parental leave: N weeks]
- [Company holidays: N days + flexible personal holidays]

**Growth**
- [Learning budget: $X/year]
- [Conference attendance: [policy]]
- [Direct access to founders and senior team]

**Equipment**
- MacBook Pro (or equivalent) provided
- [Any additional equipment: monitor, desk setup, peripherals]
```

### Section 6: Our Interview Process

**Goal:** Reduce candidate anxiety and set clear expectations. Companies that describe their process get higher response rates and better-prepared candidates.

```
## Our Interview Process

We designed our process to be thorough but respectful of your time.
Here's exactly what to expect:

**Stage 1: Recruiter conversation (30 min)**
We'll talk about your background, what you're looking for, and give you a real
picture of the role and company. You should leave this conversation knowing
whether you want to continue — and so should we.

**Stage 2: Hiring manager conversation (45–60 min)**
A deeper conversation about your experience and the role. Technical for technical roles,
strategic for leadership roles. This is also a chance to ask harder questions about
the company, team, and what success looks like.

**Stage 3: Technical or work sample (60–90 min)**
[Describe the format honestly: take-home, live coding, system design, case study, etc.]
We'll tell you exactly what to expect beforehand. We don't do trick questions or
obscure puzzles — we assess for the skills you'd actually use.

**Stage 4: Virtual onsite (3–4 hours)**
You'll meet [N] members of the team. Each conversation has a defined focus.
We'll send you the full agenda and interviewer names in advance.

**Decision & offer**
We debrief within 24–48 hours of your onsite. We don't let decisions drag.
If we're moving forward, we'll schedule a call to walk through the offer together.

**Timeline**
We aim to complete the process in [2–3] weeks from first conversation to offer.
We'll communicate clearly at every step — including if something is running slower than expected.
```

## Maintenance Checklist

**Weekly:**
- [ ] Open roles section matches ATS (no stale or filled roles listed)
- [ ] Application links work

**Monthly:**
- [ ] Team section updated if new hires joined
- [ ] Benefits section accurate (any policy changes?)
- [ ] Traction/stage line updated ("Series A" → "Series B" etc.)

**Quarterly:**
- [ ] Full page review with founders
- [ ] Candidate feedback incorporated (what questions do candidates ask that the page should answer?)
- [ ] Analytics reviewed (bounce rate, time on page, click-through to roles)
