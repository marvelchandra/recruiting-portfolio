# Job Description Writing Guide — JDs That Attract the Right People and Repel the Wrong Ones

**Maintained by:** Chandra Buduri · [linkedin.com/in/chandrabuduri](https://linkedin.com/in/chandrabuduri)

> A job description is a filter, a pitch, and a brand impression all at once. Most startup JDs fail at all three. They're too long, too generic, and written to describe a job rather than attract a person. This guide fixes that.

---

## The Purpose of a JD

A JD has exactly three jobs:

1. **Attract** candidates who are genuinely a fit
2. **Repel** candidates who aren't (wasted time on both sides)
3. **Sell** the opportunity to the ones who are on the fence

Most JDs only do job 1 poorly, skip job 2 entirely, and actively fail at job 3.

## JD Templates by Function

### Template 1: Software / ML Engineer

```markdown
# [Job Title] — [Company Name]

## About [Company]
[2–3 sentences. What you build, who uses it, what stage you're at, one investor or 
notable customer if relevant. No buzzwords.]

Example:
"[Company] builds [specific thing] for [specific customer].
We're Series A, backed by [notable investor], and [N] of the top [Y] [customers] 
use us in production. We're a team of [N] — mostly engineers, moving fast."

## The role
[2–3 sentences on what makes this specific role interesting RIGHT NOW.
What problem will this person own? What's the scope? Why is this the right moment?]

Example:
"We're at the stage where our prototype works but our infrastructure doesn't scale.
You'd be the first engineer focused entirely on our serving layer —
owning it from architecture through production, with a direct line to the CTO."

## What you'll do
- [Outcome-oriented bullet 1 — what you'll own or deliver]
- [Outcome-oriented bullet 2]
- [Outcome-oriented bullet 3]
- [Outcome-oriented bullet 4]
- [Outcome-oriented bullet 5]

Keep to 5–7 bullets. If you have more, you haven't prioritized.

## What we're looking for
Must-haves only. If you'd still consider someone without it, remove it.

- [Genuine dealbreaker 1]
- [Genuine dealbreaker 2]
- [Genuine dealbreaker 3]

## Nice to have
- [Real preference 1 — not a hidden requirement]
- [Real preference 2]

## Compensation
- **Base salary:** $[X] – $[Y]
- **Equity:** [X,000 – Y,000] options ([X]% of company at current cap table)
- **Benefits:** [Health/dental/vision], [remote stipend if remote], [any standout perks]
- **Location:** [Remote / Hybrid (N days in [city]) / Onsite ([city])]

## Our interview process
1. Recruiter screen — 30 min
2. Hiring manager conversation — 45 min
3. Technical assessment — [format, duration]
4. Virtual onsite — [N] hours
5. References + offer

We aim to complete the process in [X] weeks.
```

### Template 2: GTM / Sales Role

```markdown
# [Job Title] — [Company Name]

## About [Company]
[Same as above — short, specific, honest about stage]

## The opportunity
[What's the market opportunity this person will be selling into?
What's the ICP? What's the ACV? What does the pipeline look like?
What's exciting about the GTM motion right now?]

Example:
"We've closed our first [N] enterprise customers with zero outbound —
all inbound from word of mouth. Now we're building the outbound motion.
This is the first AE hire — you'll define the playbook, not inherit one."

## What you'll do
- Own [territory / segment / named accounts]
- [Specific GTM responsibility 1]
- [Specific GTM responsibility 2]
- [Revenue target context: "We're targeting $[X]ARR by end of year"]
- [Cross-functional work: "Partner with solutions engineering on..."]

## What we're looking for
- [Specific experience required — e.g. "closed $[X]+ enterprise deals"]
- [Domain knowledge if required — e.g. "sold into [buyer persona]"]
- [Any genuine dealbreaker]

## Compensation
- **Base salary:** $[X] – $[Y]
- **OTE:** $[X] – $[Y] ([X/Y] split base/variable)
- **Equity:** [X,000 – Y,000] options
- **Accelerators:** [Describe if any]
- **Location:** [Remote / Hybrid / Onsite]

## Our interview process
[Same as above — be specific]
```

### Template 3: Research Scientist / ML Researcher

```markdown
# [Job Title] — [Company Name]

## About [Company]
[Short, specific. Include the technical problem domain prominently.]

## The research agenda
[This section replaces "The role" for research positions.
What are the open research questions this person will work on?
What's the scientific approach? What infrastructure exists?
What's the publication policy?]

Example:
"We're working on [specific research problem]. The core question is [specific question].
We have [describe compute resources / dataset access / team structure].
We publish — papers from this team have appeared at NeurIPS, ICML, and ICLR.
You'd have significant autonomy over research direction within the broader focus area."

## What you'll work on
[Research areas, not tasks. Give enough technical depth that a researcher
can evaluate whether this is genuinely interesting work.]

## What we're looking for
- [PhD in relevant field / equivalent research experience — be specific about "equivalent"]
- [Specific technical background — methods, not tools]
- [Publication record expectations — be honest, not aspirational]

## What we offer
- Research compute: [Describe — GPU cluster, cloud budget, etc.]
- Publication policy: [Publish freely / publish with approval / IP stays internal]
- Collaboration: [External researchers, conference attendance, etc.]
- Equity: [Options, range]

## Compensation
[Same structure, but include research-specific benefits: conference budget, 
publication bonuses if any, compute access]
```

## Bias Detection Reference

### Gendered Language to Remove

| Biased term | Neutral alternative |
|---|---|
| "Aggressive" | "Driven," "results-oriented" |
| "Dominant" | "Strong," "experienced" |
| "Competitive" | "High-performing" |
| "Independent" | "Self-directed" |
| "Ninja / rockstar / guru" | The actual title |
| "Nurturing" | Remove entirely or be specific |
| "Collaborative" (overused) | "Works closely with X team on Y" |
| "Manpower" | "Workforce," "team" |
| "Chairman" | "Chair" |

### Credentialism to Remove

| Inflated requirement | Fix |
|---|---|
| "BS/MS/PhD required" | Remove unless legally required; use "equivalent experience" |
| "10+ years of Python" | Python has existed for ~30 years; use level descriptors |
| "Experience at a FAANG company" | "Experience working at high-bar engineering organizations" |
| "Stanford/MIT/CMU preferred" | Remove entirely |
| "Must have shipped X product" | Describe the capability, not the credential |

### Exclusionary Language to Remove

| Exclusionary | Inclusive alternative |
|---|---|
| "Recent graduate" | Describe the experience level you need |
| "Young, energetic team" | Describe the working style |
| "Native English speaker" | "Strong written and verbal communication" |
| "Cultural fit" | "Values alignment" + describe the specific values |
| "Must be local" | State the actual requirement (onsite X days/week) |

## JD Review Process

Before posting any JD:

**Step 1: Hiring manager review**
- Are the must-haves genuine dealbreakers?
- Are the responsibilities accurate and complete?
- Is the comp range approved?

**Step 2: Bias check**
- Run through Gender Decoder or equivalent
- Review against the bias checklist above
- Ask: would a qualified candidate from any background find this welcoming?

**Step 3: Recruiter review**
- Is this sellable? Would you be excited to pitch this role?
- Is the comp competitive (check against comp benchmarking doc)?
- Does it accurately represent the role (sets right expectations)?

**Step 4: Founder spot-check (for senior roles)**
- Does the "about" section accurately represent the company?
- Is the mission framing compelling and honest?
- Any claims that need updating (funding, customer count, team size)?
